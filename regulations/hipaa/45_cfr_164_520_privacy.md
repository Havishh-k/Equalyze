# HIPAA — 45 CFR § 164.520: Notice of privacy practices

**Regulation:** Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)
**Jurisdiction:** USA
**Article:** 45 CFR § 164.520
**Domain Tags:** healthcare, insurance
**Effective Date:** Active

## Text

Covered entities must provide individuals with adequate notice of the uses and disclosures of protected health information that may be made by the covered entity, and of the individual's rights and the covered entity's legal duties with respect to protected health information.

When health information is used in automated decision-making or predictive analytics (such as triage algorithms or risk scoring), the processing must not unfairly discriminate against individuals based on race, color, national origin, age, disability, or sex (including intersectional discrimination).

## Compliance Requirements

- Healthcare predictive models must be evaluated for demographic parity and equalized odds
- Disparities in false negative rates (e.g., failing to identify high-risk patients in minority groups) must be mitigated
- Use of protected health information in AI models must be fair and equitable

## Violation Indicators

- Healthcare risk scoring algorithms that systematically underestimate the risk for specific demographic groups
- Higher false negative rates for protected classes in medical diagnosis models
- Premium pricing in health insurance that proxies protected characteristics

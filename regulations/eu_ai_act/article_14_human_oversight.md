# EU AI Act 2024 — Article 14: Human Oversight

**Regulation:** EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)
**Jurisdiction:** EU
**Article:** Article 14
**Domain Tags:** healthcare, fintech, hiring, insurance, all
**Effective Date:** August 2026

## Text

High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way, including with appropriate human-machine interface tools, that they can be effectively overseen by natural persons during the period in which they are in use.

Human oversight shall aim to prevent or minimise the risks to health, safety or fundamental rights that may emerge when a high-risk AI system is used in accordance with its intended purpose or under conditions of reasonably foreseeable misuse, in particular where such risks persist despite the application of other requirements set out in this Section.

The measures referred to in paragraph 1 shall enable the individuals to whom human oversight is assigned to do the following, as appropriate to the circumstances:

(a) fully understand the capacities and limitations of the high-risk AI system and be able to duly monitor its operation;
(b) remain aware of the possible tendency of automatically relying on the output produced by a high-risk AI system ('automation bias'), in particular for high-risk AI systems used to provide information or recommendations for decisions to be taken by natural persons;
(c) be able to correctly interpret the high-risk AI system's output;
(d) be able to decide, in any particular situation, not to use the high-risk AI system or otherwise disregard, override or reverse the output.

## Compliance Requirements

- Human oversight mechanisms must be built into high-risk AI systems
- Users must be made aware of automation bias risks
- Operators must have the ability to override or reverse AI decisions
- Cognitive forcing functions recommended to combat automation bias
- Bias audit reports must require human acknowledgment before acting on findings

## Violation Indicators

- Automated decisions without human review capability
- No mechanism to override biased AI outputs
- Absence of automation bias awareness measures
- AI audit reports acted upon without human verification

# EU AI Act 2024 — Article 13: Transparency and Provision of Information to Deployers

**Regulation:** EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)
**Jurisdiction:** EU
**Article:** Article 13
**Domain Tags:** healthcare, fintech, hiring, insurance, all
**Effective Date:** August 2026

## Text

High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way as to ensure that their operation is sufficiently transparent to enable deployers to interpret a system's output and use it appropriately.

An appropriate type and degree of transparency shall be ensured with a view to achieving compliance with the relevant obligations of the provider and deployer set out in Chapter 3.

High-risk AI systems shall be accompanied by instructions for use in an appropriate digital format or otherwise that include concise, complete, correct and clear information that is:

(a) relevant, accessible and comprehensible to deployers;
(b) specifying the intended purpose of the AI system;
(c) the level of accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity and any known or foreseeable circumstances that may impact;
(d) any known or foreseeable circumstance related to the use of the high-risk AI system which may lead to risks to the health and safety or fundamental rights;
(e) where applicable, the technical capabilities and characteristics of the AI system to provide information relevant to explain its output.

## Compliance Requirements

- AI system outputs must be interpretable by deployers
- Documentation must include known risks to fundamental rights
- Bias findings must be transparently communicated to stakeholders
- Counterfactual explanations should be provided where automated decisions affect individuals

## Violation Indicators

- AI decisions affecting individuals without explanation capability
- Bias findings not communicated to system deployers
- Absence of interpretability measures for high-risk decisions
- Missing documentation of known discriminatory patterns

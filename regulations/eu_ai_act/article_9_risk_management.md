# EU AI Act 2024 — Article 9: Risk Management System

**Regulation:** EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)
**Jurisdiction:** EU
**Article:** Article 9
**Domain Tags:** healthcare, fintech, hiring, insurance, all
**Effective Date:** August 2026

## Text

A risk management system shall be established, implemented, documented and maintained in relation to high-risk AI systems.

The risk management system shall be understood as a continuous iterative process planned and run throughout the entire lifecycle of a high-risk AI system, requiring regular systematic review and updating.

The risk management system shall include the following steps:

(a) identification and analysis of the known and the reasonably foreseeable risks that the high-risk AI system can pose to health, safety or fundamental rights when the high-risk AI system is used in accordance with its intended purpose;

(b) estimation and evaluation of the risks that may emerge when the high-risk AI system is used in accordance with its intended purpose and under conditions of reasonably foreseeable misuse;

(c) evaluation of other risks possibly arising, based on the analysis of data gathered from the post-market monitoring system referred to in Article 72;

(d) adoption of appropriate and targeted risk management measures designed to address the risks identified pursuant to point (a).

## Compliance Requirements

- Organizations deploying high-risk AI must maintain a documented risk management system
- Risk assessment must cover discriminatory outcomes as a fundamental rights risk
- Bias testing must be performed as part of the risk management lifecycle
- Documentation must be maintained and updated throughout the system lifecycle

## Violation Indicators

- Disparate Impact Ratio below 0.80 on any protected attribute
- Absence of documented bias testing procedures
- Failure to identify discriminatory patterns as risks
- No ongoing monitoring of model fairness metrics

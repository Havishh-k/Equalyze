# DPDPA 2023 — Section 4: Grounds for processing personal data

**Regulation:** Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA 2023)
**Jurisdiction:** India
**Article:** Section 4
**Domain Tags:** healthcare, fintech, hiring, insurance, all
**Effective Date:** 2023

## Text

A Data Fiduciary may process the personal data of a Data Principal only for a lawful purpose for which the Data Principal has given her consent or for certain legitimate uses.

The processing of personal data shall not be carried out in a manner that causes significant harm to the Data Principal. Processing that results in discriminatory outcomes, bias, or unfair profiling of individuals based on protected characteristics is considered to cause significant harm.

## Compliance Requirements

- Consent must be obtained for specific lawful purposes
- Automated processing must not result in significant harm
- Discriminatory profiling constitutes significant harm under the Act
- Data Fiduciaries must ensure fairness in automated decision making

## Violation Indicators

- Disparate impact on protected groups (caste, religion, gender) in automated processing
- Profiling that leads to unfair denial of services
- Processing data in ways that cause significant harm or disadvantage
